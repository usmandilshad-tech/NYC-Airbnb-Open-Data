1. Room Types Distribution

Over 52% of listings in NYC are for entire homes or apartments, followed by private rooms at around 45%, and a small fraction (2.2%) offering shared rooms.
Interpretation: This suggests that the NYC Airbnb market leans toward privacy and comfort, with hosts targeting longer-term stays or travelers who prefer full-unit access. Shared rooms are rare, indicating either low demand or policy-driven limitations in offering shared spaces.

2. Listings by Borough

Manhattan and Brooklyn have the highest number of listings, each exceeding 16,000, while Queens has around 4,500. Bronx and Staten Island have significantly fewer listings.
Interpretation: This shows a geographic skew of Airbnb activity in NYC, closely aligned with tourist hotspots and economic centers. For Airbnb investors or city planners, it indicates where regulations or competition are most intense.

3. Average Price by Borough

Manhattan leads with the highest average nightly rate, followed by Brooklyn and Queens. The Bronx and Staten Island are the most budget-friendly.
Interpretation: Pricing aligns with NYC's cost-of-living index and tourism demand. This could guide travelers on budgeting or help hosts benchmark competitive pricing based on location.
